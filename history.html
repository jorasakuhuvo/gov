History of the brave Vasians and their Jorasakuhuvo

Before Everything Started

Previously, the people who later became Vasians were all Han Chinese. They had lived in mainland China for a long time, and their culture and lifestyle were no different from those of others. They did not develop strong opinions about the political system. For them, China was their homeland, and they intended to remain there. Barring unforeseen circumstances, their future would not have been much different from that of ordinary people.

Contacted the World

In the 2020s, an opportunity to study abroad allowed some of them to access software banned in China for the first time, revealing previously unavailable information. Through this information, they began to re-examine the country and political system they had always trusted. Some also learned that their relatives had been robbed of their hard-earned wealth during past political movements. These experiences and memories accumulated, ultimately leading to profound reflection.

On the night of April 13, 2025, they made a collective commitment: to build a fair, equal, democratic, peaceful, and free place for their compatriots. From that moment on, the former Han Chinese community began to identify with a new identity, and a nation called "Vasians" was born.

The Road to Establishment

Building a completely new nation from scratch was a daunting task, with no one knowing how to proceed. Fortunately, someone offered their home, becoming the first territory of this new nation. Subsequently, a language, currency, and new values ​​were gradually established. On June 7, 2025, the arrangement and recording of "Potonyhuni Pe Vasok" (The Vasians' Song) were completed. The lyrics were written later and underwent several revisions.

As time passed, history entered the second quarter of the 21st century. On January 29, 2026, Pemife Noraane, elected as president, announced to the world on the website: The Provisional Government of Jorasakuhuvo was officially established.

Towards the Future

On February 2, 2026, three days after the founding of the nation, Pemife Noraane declared that Jorasakuhuvo's airspace was open only to the military. On February 6, Jorasakuhuvian was established as the official language of the country and used alongside English, the internationally spoken language.

On February 11, after violating a previously forced treaty, the Chinese group was accused by Jorasakuhuvo of territorial incursions. Jorasakuhuvo released relevant evidence and gained the moral high ground on the issue. The Chinese group subsequently stated that they were unable to continue imposing restrictions on Jorasakuhuvo, an event seen as a significant obstacle for the Vasians.

On February 12, Thai representatives held talks with Jorasakuhuvo. During the talks, the representatives stated, "Jorasakuhuvo is a very interesting country…it's a good thing for the Vasians."

On February 14, the Chinese group re-entered Jorasakuhuvo territory without issuing a statement and refused to respond.

On February 17, a Taiwanese representative stated during a conversation with Jorasakuhuvo, "Jorasakuhuvo is very impressive... their courage and hard work are admirable."

On February 18, the Chinese delegation returned to the negotiating table with Jorasakuhuvo and normalized relations.

Jorasakuhuvo continued to develop and became what it is today.
